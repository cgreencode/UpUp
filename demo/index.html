A tiny script that makes sure your site is always there for your users

(even when they're on a plane over the Atlantic, or 20,000 leagues under the sea)

Imagine opening TripAdvisor on your laptop and getting the top 5 attractions in Rome, half an hour after take-off from JFK.

Imagine opening Booking.com on your tablet and seeing the address for your hotel reservation, while on the cab from the airport.

Imagine checking your corporate site from your phone in that one spot in your house where the wi-fi is never good enough (you know the place) and getting a list of your upcoming meetings for the day.

Want proof? Disconnect from the internet and reload this page… we promise it will blow you away!

The days when you could count on your visitors sitting at their desktops, with a wire connecting them to the internet are long gone. Today, your users are visiting your site from a phone in the elevator, from their tablet on the subway, or on their laptop while they sit cramped into seat 25 E.

Luckily, modern browsers support technologies that let you serve your content to your users, even when they are offline.

UpUp makes all of this modern magic as simple as calling one command.


<script src="upup.min.js"></script>
<script>
  UpUp.start({
    'content-url': 'offline.html' // show this to page to offline users
  });
</script>

It's time to start the offline-first revolution.
